The frame I used was a little deep, so I laid some slices of cardboard below the mat.Â  This way, the mat would get pressed against the glass instead of wiggling around.Â  Then, I cut more slices of card board in a sort of L shape to fit around the phone.Â  Next, I glued it all together, making sure that the slices were aligned.

While the glue dried, I took the frame into the garage and gave it a good coat of spray paint.Â  I wanted to use a black paint to bring out the image, but all I had on hand was primer gray and a hammered finished metallic paint.Â  I didn't feel like packing up the kid and drive to Lowe's, so I used the hammered finish stuff.Â  Anyway, one good coat, then another a few hours later.Â  It was dry by the next day and <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/theguamaso/3388221446/in/set-72157615939909968/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">looked pretty good</a>.

The "pocket" should be ready by the next day, too.Â  Although, the glue I used constricted the cardboard a bit after it dried - I should have left something inside the pocket to push against the cardboard.Â  Oh well.
